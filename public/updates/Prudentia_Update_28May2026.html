The Sprint Test: Prudentia Returns to Te Rapa

PRUDENTIA DURING TRACK WORK AT WEXFORD STABLES, PREPARING FOR HER SPRINT TEST AT TE RAPA.

A tactical shift sees our BM65 Te Rapa winner return to the Waikato turf this Saturday, dropping back to the 1200m sprint trip in peak physical condition.

Prudentia returns to the gates at Te Rapa this Saturday, 30 May, dropping back to a 1200m trip for the Roy and Pam Browne Sprint (BM75).

“She is sound and in great order. If we get some rain to put cut in the track, she will be highly competitive.”
— Andrew Scott, Wexford Stables

Following a gutsy third-place finish over 1400m on May 2—where she hit the line fastest in the final 100m—trainers Lance O'Sullivan and Andrew Scott have elected to drop her back in distance. This shift keeps her sharp, leveraging the strong tactical turn of foot she showed during her Te Rapa win in April.

The stable reports she is in peak physical order, trotting up soundly after clocking a steady 600m in 43.1 seconds last Saturday. Carrying a light 54.0kg from Barrier 1, she is perfectly positioned to save ground along the rail. We are monitoring the weather closely; any late-week rain will play heavily to her strengths.
