The Sprint Test: Prudentia Returns to Te Rapa

Prudentia returns to the gates at Te Rapa this Saturday, 30 May, dropping back to a 1200m trip for the Roy and Pam Browne Sprint (BM75).

Following a gutsy third-place finish over 1400m on May 2—where she hit the line fastest in the final 100m—trainers Lance O'Sullivan and Andrew Scott have elected to drop her back in distance. This shift keeps her sharp, leveraging the strong tactical turn of foot she showed during her Te Rapa win in April.

Why This Race Sets Up Well

01
Inside Advantage Barrier 1

Drawing Barrier 1 allows Prudentia to save ground on the fence, a tactic she used to perfection in her last placing at Te Rapa.

She has taken immense physical improvement since her last start on May 2, trotting up sound and showing plenty of energy in her recent track work.

03
Tactical Distance Drop 1200m

Dropping back to 1200m keeps her sharp and allows her to make a sustained, powerful run down the long Te Rapa straight.

04
Weight Relief 54.0kg

Carrying just 54.0kg gives her a major advantage against higher-rated runners in the Benchmark 75 grade.
