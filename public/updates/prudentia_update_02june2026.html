INVESTOR UPDATE: PRUDENTIA

Te Rapa Review

Prudentia put in a massive effort at Te Rapa on Saturday, though her fifth-place finish doesn’t tell the full story.

PRUDENTIA, WITH JOCKEY MASA HASHIZUME IN THE SADDLE, RETURNING FROM HER SPRINT TEST AT TE RAPA.

Jumping cleanly from Barrier 1, she was immediately positioned on the fence. As the field bunched around the bend, she became pocketed along the rail, leaving her with nowhere to stretch her stride. She traveled comfortably but spent the straight held up behind a wall of horses, only finding a sliver of daylight in the final 100 meters. Once clear, she surged through the line with impressive late speed to finish close behind the placegetters.

“She trotted up soundly this morning and scoped completely clean. The rail race was frustrating, but her final 100 metres showed massive tenacity. She is in superb order.”
— Andrew Scott, Wexford Stables

Despite the frustrating rail run, her final-furlong acceleration proved she has the class and versatility to compete at this Benchmark 75 level. Once a sliver of daylight appeared, she finished with high-velocity sectionals, surging through the line. It was a run of quiet authority that leaves the team highly encouraged for her winter campaign.

This morning, 2 June, the Wexford yard conducted a comprehensive post-race assessment. Trainers Lance O'Sullivan and Andrew Scott report that she has trotted up 100% soundly. An endoscopy scoped completely clean, showing clear airways, and she has fully cleared her feed box. Because she was so heavily held up, she didn't get to have a hard run and comes out of the weekend in peak fitness. Lance and Andrew plan to keep her active, targeting another Benchmark 75 event over 1200m or 1400m in late June, where her heavy-track pedigree will serve her well.
