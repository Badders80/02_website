  Investor Update — Prudentia

Tauranga Review: Testing Ground

She finished sixth in Race 7 on Saturday — tried hard on ground that turned puggier through the card and never quite suited her stride.

Prudentia — sixth, 4.59L · Barrier 1 · 54kg · Masa Hashizume

From Barrier 1, Prudentia jumped cleanly with Masa Hashizume in the saddle. Rocking won by 0.89 lengths; our mare crossed the line 4.59 lengths back in sixth. The card was run on Heavy 10 footing, but as the afternoon wore on the surface grew puggier and stickier — a very different proposition from the looser ground on which she broke her maiden here last May. Masa stayed on the rail where the winners were coming from, yet it proved a day when ground was hard to make. Even in the prelim, Kyle Bax noted she was not hitting out as she normally does.

 
“She tried really hard — but today she just found that ground a little bit too testing. It was really holding. What she won here last time was pretty loose.”
— Andrew Scott, Wexford Stables

Masa reported that every time she tried to stretch, she got stuck in the mud. Andrew Scott confirms she had a blow post-race, though the effort will do her good. Kyle Bax reports she is fine this evening. If she has a good week, the yard will look to back her up this coming Saturday at Te Rapa — BM75 over 1400m, a track she has already won on. Watch for nominations on Tuesday.
